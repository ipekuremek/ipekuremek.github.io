Chaos and Consciousness

Imagine a universe not of stars and galaxies, but of neurons. Each neuron represents not a point in space, but a dimension unto itself— a unique realm of possibility within the vast cosmos of the mind. In this universe, the brain is not a three-dimensional organ but a multi-dimensional odyssey, with 86 billion dimensions and almost infinite potentiality. In this analogy, if consciousness is linked to neuronal activity, this model provides a representation of consciousness that can be analyzed.

In this multiverse, each spark of activity from a single neuron—a dimension—is a note in the silent music of thought and consciousness. When one neuron fires, it does not echo in the emptiness of space but vibrates through countless other dimensions, each one influencing and being influenced by the rest. It's as if every idea, every memory, every sensation is a starburst of activity, illuminating the neural cosmos with complex patterns.

The interactions we navigate in every moment of our existence, often without realizing is the complexity beneath our most simple actions. Every decision we make, every emotion we feel, is not just a point on a map, but a path through a dimension. The concept of chaos in this neural multiverse is the unpredictability of moving through these dimensions. It is the recognition that the path from one thought to another is not linear or fixed, but is a wandering through the endless possibilities of our inner worlds. Our behavior is not a single storyline but a choose-your-own-adventure spread across an infinite array of dimensions, each neuron a crossroads of choice and chance.

What conclusions can we draw from this point? First, the path is chaotic. The system can be deterministic, but the behavior of it is unpredictable. So, we can never predict how people will behave. Whilst being chaotic, it is not random. It follows a strange attractor called "personality".

In this analogy, to understand the human behavior is to understand the paths through these dimensions—to map the uncharted territories of the neural multiverse. It is to recognize that within the brain's vastness lies not just the mystery of who we are, but the revelation of what we might become. In the universe of possibilities that define human behavior and identity, discovering oneself might seem very chaotic, yet we see that even chaos contains its own cosmos, its own order within. The human mind, dependent on thousands of characteristic parameters, makes its future choices based on these parameters. Understanding these parameters not only brings us closer to the cosmos within the chaos, but will also show that the cosmos comes from chaos, and chaos comes from the cosmos. The book that inspired this writing is 'Introducing Chaos: A Graphic Guide'.
